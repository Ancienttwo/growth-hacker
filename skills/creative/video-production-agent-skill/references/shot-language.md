# Shot Language Guide

Use precise, observable language.

## Prefer

- “A waist-level 35 mm lateral track follows CHAR-001 two meters from right to left.”
- “PROP-001 remains closed in the character’s left hand until it is placed on the bench.”
- “The shot begins with the bench on the left third and ends on an empty locked frame.”

## Avoid

- “Cinematic camera.”
- “Epic mood.”
- “The character does several things and the world transforms.”
- “Keep it consistent.”

## Camera fields

- **Shot size:** ECU, CU, MCU, MS, MLS, WS, EWS, insert, over-shoulder, POV.
- **Angle:** eye level, high, low, overhead, dutch, profile, three-quarter.
- **Lens:** focal length or a bounded optical character; avoid contradictory lens descriptions.
- **Movement:** locked, pan, tilt, dolly, track, crane, handheld, steadicam, orbit, push-in, pull-out.
- **Composition:** subject placement, foreground/midground/background, headroom, lead room, geometry, screen direction.

One shot may contain multiple micro-movements only when they form one continuous action and preserve a clear first/last state.
